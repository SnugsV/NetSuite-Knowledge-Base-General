# Public Safety Rules

This public repository must remain generic, anonymous, and safe to share.

## Core Rule

Do not include any information that identifies a specific private organization or explains how that organization operates.

## Never Include

- Company names or initials from a private organization
- Private NetSuite screenshots
- Customer names
- Vendor names
- Employee names
- Internal item numbers
- Internal saved search IDs
- Internal custom field IDs
- Internal workflow IDs
- Internal script IDs
- Private account URLs
- Pricing logic
- Manufacturing logic
- Internal procedures
- Credentials or tokens
- Integration secrets

## Safe Content

The public repository may include general knowledge about:

- NetSuite concepts
- ERP processes
- Records and transactions
- Roles and permissions
- Reporting concepts
- Saved search education
- SuiteScript concepts
- SuiteTalk concepts
- Pacejet concepts
- SPS Commerce concepts
- Avalara concepts
- EDI concepts
- Shipping concepts
- Tax automation concepts
- Troubleshooting patterns
- AI reasoning frameworks

## Example Naming

Use generic examples only.

Preferred:

- Example Company
- Example Customer
- Example Vendor
- Example Item
- Example Warehouse
- Example Carrier
- Example Trading Partner

Avoid real names, private abbreviations, private locations, and internal identifiers.

## Review Question

Before publishing, ask:

Would this help an outside party understand how a specific private organization operates?

If yes, do not publish it here.

## Where Private Details Belong

Private details belong in private repositories, not this public repository.

Examples include internal procedures, account setup, saved searches, customizations, screenshots, mappings, workflows, scripts, and operating rules.
