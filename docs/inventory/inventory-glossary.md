# Inventory Glossary

## Quick Summary

A reference of terms used in inventory management in NetSuite.

## A

**Assembly Item**
An item type that represents a product built from components using a bill of materials (BOM).

**Available Quantity**
The quantity of an item that can be promised to customers. Calculated as On Hand minus Committed.

**Average Cost**
A valuation method where the cost of all units is averaged. Total cost divided by total quantity.

## B

**Bill of Materials (BOM)**
A list of components, quantities, and steps needed to build an assembly item.

**Bin**
A specific storage location within a warehouse (e.g., "Aisle 3, Shelf 4, Bin 12"). Used with bin management features.

**Build Order**
A transaction that authorizes the assembly of an item from its component parts.

## C

**COGS (Cost of Goods Sold)**
The cost of inventory that has been sold or consumed. Recorded as an expense on the income statement.

**Committed Quantity**
Inventory that has been allocated to sales orders, work orders, or other commitments. This stock is "spoken for" but not yet shipped.

**Cycle Count**
An ongoing inventory verification process where a subset of items is counted on a regular schedule.

## F

**FIFO (First In, First Out)**
A valuation method where the oldest units are assumed to be sold first. The cost follows the physical flow of goods.

**Fulfillment**
The process of picking, packing, and shipping items from inventory to fill a sales order.

## I

**Inventory Adjustment**
A transaction that increases or decreases inventory quantity, typically for corrections, damages, shrinkage, or write-offs.

**Inventory Item**
An item type used for physical products that are stocked, tracked, and valued.

**Inventory Transfer**
A transaction that moves inventory between locations in a single step.

**Item Receipt**
A transaction that records inventory received from a vendor against a purchase order.

**Item Record**
The master data record for a product, part, material, or service in NetSuite.

**Item Type**
The classification of an item that determines its behavior (Inventory, Non-Inventory, Service, Kit, Assembly, etc.).

## K

**Kit/Package Item**
An item type that groups multiple items into a pre-configured bundle for sale.

## L

**Landed Cost**
The total cost of inventory including purchase price, freight, insurance, duties, and other acquisition costs.

**LIFO (Last In, First Out)**
A valuation method where the newest units are assumed to be sold first.

**Location**
A place where inventory is stored, such as a warehouse, store, dock, or truck.

## M

**Multi-Location Inventory**
A NetSuite feature that enables tracking inventory quantities by location.

## N

**Non-Inventory Item**
An item type for products that are bought and sold without tracking quantities or cost in NetSuite.

## O

**On Hand Quantity**
The physical quantity of an item currently in stock at all locations.

**On Order Quantity**
The quantity of an item expected from purchase orders that have not yet been received.

## P

**Physical Inventory Count**
A complete count of all inventory items, typically performed annually for financial reporting.

**Purchase Unit**
The unit of measure used when purchasing an item from a supplier. Must be convertible to the stock unit.

## R

**Reorder Point**
The minimum quantity of an item at which a replenishment order should be placed.

## S

**Sales Unit**
The unit of measure used when selling an item to a customer. Must be convertible to the stock unit.

**Service Item**
An item type used for labor, consulting, or professional services that are billed to customers.

**Standard Cost**
A valuation method where a fixed cost is assigned to an item. Variances between standard and actual cost are recorded separately.

**Stock Unit**
The base unit of measure for quantity tracking. All inventory quantities are stored in this unit.

## T

**Transfer Order**
A tracked inventory movement between locations that goes through stages (pending, shipped, received, in transit).

## U

**UOM (Unit of Measure)**
The unit used to count, purchase, store, or sell an item. Items can have multiple UOMs with conversion rates.

## V

**Valuation Method**
The method used to calculate the cost of inventory (Average Cost, Standard Cost, FIFO, LIFO, Specific Cost).
