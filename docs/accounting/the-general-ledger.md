# The General Ledger

## Quick Summary

The General Ledger (GL) is the central repository of all financial transactions. Every transaction — from a purchase order to a customer payment — ultimately appears in the GL. The Chart of Accounts defines how transactions are classified.

## Difficulty

Beginner

## Estimated Time

15 minutes

## Business Question

"Where does every transaction end up, and how do I find it?"

## Overview

The GL is the complete record of all financial activity. Think of the Chart of Accounts as the filing cabinet and the GL as the filing system. Every transaction creates journal entries that update GL account balances.

## Chart of Accounts

The Chart of Accounts (COA) is the list of all accounts used to classify transactions:

| Account Type | Examples | Financial Statement |
|---|---|---|
| **Assets** | Cash, AR, Inventory, Equipment | Balance Sheet |
| **Liabilities** | AP, Accrued Expenses, Deferred Revenue | Balance Sheet |
| **Equity** | Retained Earnings, Common Stock | Balance Sheet |
| **Revenue** | Sales Revenue, Service Revenue | Income Statement |
| **Expenses** | COGS, Salaries, Rent | Income Statement |

## Debits and Credits

Every journal entry has equal debits and credits:

| Account Type | Debit Increases | Credit Increases |
|---|---|---|
| Asset | ✓ | |
| Liability | | ✓ |
| Equity | | ✓ |
| Revenue | | ✓ |
| Expense | ✓ | |

## Subledgers

Subledgers contain detail for specific GL accounts:

| Subledger | Transactions | GL Account |
|---|---|---|
| **AP Subledger** | Vendor bills, payments | Accounts Payable |
| **AR Subledger** | Invoices, customer payments | Accounts Receivable |
| **Inventory Subledger** | Receipts, fulfillments, adjustments | Inventory Asset |
| **Fixed Asset Subledger** | Asset additions, depreciation | Fixed Assets |

## Business Example

A vendor bill for $1,000 is entered. The AP subledger records the bill detail. A journal entry posts to the GL: Debit Expense $1,000, Credit AP $1,000. When the bill is paid, another entry: Debit AP $1,000, Credit Cash $1,000. The GL always shows the net effect.
