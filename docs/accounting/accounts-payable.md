# Accounts Payable

## Quick Summary

Accounts Payable (AP) processes vendor bills and issues payments to suppliers. AP is the financial counterpart to the purchasing process — while purchasing negotiates and orders, AP pays.

## Difficulty

Intermediate

## Estimated Time

10 minutes

## Overview

AP is where operational procurement meets financial accounting. Every vendor bill that passes through AP creates a liability on the balance sheet and an expense or asset on the income statement. AP impacts cash flow, vendor relationships, and financial reporting accuracy.

## AP Flow

```
Vendor Bill Received
       ↓
Matched to PO and Receipt
       ↓
Approved for Payment
       ↓
Scheduled for Payment (based on terms)
       ↓
Payment Processed
       ↓
Cash Reduced, AP Reduced
```

## Common Customer Questions

**Q**: A vendor bill was entered but the payment was not created. What should I check? **Records to Inspect**: Bill status (approved?), payment terms, due date, vendor payment method, available cash.

**Q**: Why is AP balance higher than expected? **Records to Inspect**: Unpaid bills, bills not yet approved, duplicate bills, unapplied credits.
