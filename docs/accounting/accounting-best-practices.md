# Accounting Best Practices

## General Ledger

- Maintain a clean chart of accounts — review annually for inactive or duplicate accounts
- Use account segments or classes for meaningful reporting
- Document the purpose of every account in the COA
- Restrict journal entry posting to qualified accounting staff
- Review the trial balance monthly for unusual activity

## Period Close

- Maintain a documented close checklist with assigned responsibilities
- Close subledgers before the GL — AP, AR, inventory, payroll
- Reconcile all bank accounts before finalizing
- Review open period items (unposted transactions, unmatched documents)
- Target close within 5 business days of period end

## AP and AR

- Match bills to POs and receipts before entering (three-way matching)
- Reconcile AP aging weekly
- Monitor DSO monthly and follow up on overdue accounts
- Take all available early payment discounts
- Reconcile AR aging weekly and escalate aged items

## Inventory Accounting

- Reconcile inventory subledger to GL monthly
- Investigate negative on-hand quantities immediately
- Review COGS as a percentage of revenue monthly
- Perform cycle counting to maintain inventory accuracy

## System Configuration

- Enable approval workflows for journal entries above a threshold
- Configure audit trail review for key accounts
- Use saved searches for period close monitoring
- Restrict period closing permissions to authorized users
- Maintain segregation of duties between AP, AR, and GL roles

## Health Scorecard

| Metric | Healthy | Warning | Critical |
|---|---|---|---|
| Close Time | < 5 days | 5-10 days | > 10 days |
| DSO | < 45 days | 45-60 days | > 60 days |
| Inventory Turns | Industry benchmark ± 10% | ± 20% | > 20% off |
| Journal Entries per Close | < 20 | 20-50 | > 50 |
| Cash to Close Items | < 5 | 5-10 | > 10 |
