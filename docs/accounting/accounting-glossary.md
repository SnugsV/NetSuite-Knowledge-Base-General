# Accounting Glossary

## A

**Accounts Payable (AP)**
Amounts owed to vendors and suppliers for goods or services received.

**Accounts Receivable (AR)**
Amounts owed by customers for goods or services provided.

**Accrual Accounting**
Revenue and expenses recorded when earned or incurred, not when cash is exchanged.

## B

**Balance Sheet**
Financial statement showing assets, liabilities, and equity at a point in time.

## C

**Chart of Accounts (COA)**
The complete list of accounts used to classify financial transactions.

**Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)**
The direct cost of products sold during a period.

## D

**Deferred Revenue**
Cash received for goods or services not yet delivered. Recorded as a liability.

## G

**General Ledger (GL)**
The complete record of all financial transactions.

**Gross Profit**
Revenue minus COGS.

## I

**Income Statement**
Financial statement showing revenue, expenses, and profit over a period. Also called P&L.

## J

**Journal Entry**
A record of a financial transaction with equal debits and credits.

## P

**Period Close**
The process of finalizing all transactions for an accounting period.

## R

**Record-to-Report (R2R)**
The end-to-end process from recording transactions to producing financial statements.

**Revenue Recognition**
The accounting rules determining when revenue is recorded.

## S

**Subledger**
A detailed record supporting a GL account (AP subledger, AR subledger, etc.).

## T

**Trial Balance**
A listing of all GL accounts and their balances at a point in time.
