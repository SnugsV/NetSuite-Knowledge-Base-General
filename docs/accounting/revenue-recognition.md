# Revenue Recognition

## Quick Summary

Revenue recognition determines when revenue is recorded in the financial statements. Under ASC 606, revenue is recognized when performance obligations are satisfied — not necessarily when cash is received or when an invoice is sent.

## Difficulty

Advanced

## Estimated Time

15 minutes

## Business Question

"When should I record revenue for this transaction?"

## Overview

Revenue recognition rules exist because revenue should match the value delivered to the customer. A product shipped today with payment received 30 days later — the revenue belongs to today, not the payment date. A one-year subscription billed upfront — the revenue belongs to each month of the subscription, not the billing date.

## Core Principle

Under ASC 606 / IFRS 15, revenue is recognized when control of goods or services transfers to the customer. This follows a five-step model:

1. Identify the contract with the customer
2. Identify the performance obligations
3. Determine the transaction price
4. Allocate the transaction price
5. Recognize revenue when obligations are satisfied

## Recognition Methods

| Method | When Revenue Is Recognized | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **At Point in Time** | When control transfers to customer | Product shipment, delivery |
| **Over Time** | Over the service period | Subscriptions, maintenance |
| **Upon Milestone** | When contractual milestones met | Project-based services |
| **Percentage of Completion** | Based on project completion % | Long-term contracts |

## Deferred Revenue

When cash is received before revenue is earned:

| Transaction | Debit | Credit |
|---|---|---|
| Customer pays $12,000 annual subscription upfront | Cash $12,000 | Deferred Revenue $12,000 |
| Monthly revenue recognition ($1,000/month) | Deferred Revenue $1,000 | Revenue $1,000 |

## Common Customer Questions

**Q**: Revenue was recognized but it shouldn't have been yet. What should I check? **Records to Inspect**: Fulfillment records, revenue arrangement setup, item revenue rules, deferred revenue schedules.

**Q**: Deferred revenue balance seems incorrect. What should I inspect? **Records to Inspect**: Revenue recognition schedules, unfulfilled performance obligations, credit memos against deferred items.
