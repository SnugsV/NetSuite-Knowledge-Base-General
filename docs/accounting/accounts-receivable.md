# Accounts Receivable

## Quick Summary

Accounts Receivable (AR) manages customer invoices, payments, and collections. AR is the financial counterpart to the sales process — while sales generates orders and fulfillment, AR collects the cash.

## Difficulty

Intermediate

## Estimated Time

10 minutes

## Overview

AR represents money owed to the company by its customers. Every invoice creates an AR balance. Every payment reduces it. Managing AR effectively is critical for cash flow and working capital.

## AR Flow

```
Invoice Sent to Customer
       ↓
AR Created (Debit AR, Credit Revenue)
       ↓
Payment Received
       ↓
Cash Received (Debit Cash, Credit AR)
       ↓
AR Balance Reduced
```

## Common Customer Questions

**Q**: A customer paid but the invoice still shows as open. What should I check? **Records to Inspect**: Payment was applied to the correct invoice, payment method was processed, no unapplied payment exists.

**Q**: AR balance is increasing faster than revenue. What is happening? **Records to Inspect**: Collections are slowing (DSO increasing), credit memos are not being processed, invoices are being created but not collected.
