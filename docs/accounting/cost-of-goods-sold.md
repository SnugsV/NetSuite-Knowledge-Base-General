# Cost of Goods Sold

## Quick Summary

Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) represents the direct cost of products or services sold during a period. COGS is one of the most important financial metrics — it directly determines gross profit.

## Difficulty

Intermediate

## Estimated Time

10 minutes

## Overview

COGS is the expense side of the revenue equation. If a product sells for $100 and the COGS is $60, the gross profit is $40. Understanding exactly what goes into COGS — and when it is recorded — is essential for accurate financial reporting.

## What Goes into COGS

| Business Type | COGS Components |
|---|---|
| **Distribution** | Purchase cost + freight + duty |
| **Manufacturing** | Raw materials + direct labor + manufacturing overhead |
| **Assembly** | Component costs + assembly labor |

## COGS Recognition

| Transaction | COGS Impact |
|---|---|
| Inventory received | No COGS — inventory asset increases |
| Item fulfillment | COGS recorded at average/standard cost |
| Inventory adjustment | May increase or decrease COGS |
| Work order completion | Cost moves from WIP to finished goods |
