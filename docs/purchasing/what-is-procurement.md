# What Is Procurement?

## Quick Summary

Procurement is the end-to-end process of acquiring goods and services from external suppliers. It spans need identification, vendor selection, purchase order creation, receipt, invoicing, and payment. The Procure-to-Pay (P2P) lifecycle is the backbone of organizational spending.

## Difficulty

Beginner

## Estimated Time

## Overview

Every organization buys things. Raw materials for production, office supplies for daily operations, software subscriptions, consulting services, capital equipment — the list is endless. Procurement is the discipline of managing those purchases efficiently, cost-effectively, and with appropriate controls.

## The Procure-to-Pay Lifecycle

```
1. Need Identification
       ↓
2. Vendor Selection
       ↓
3. Purchase Requisition
       ↓
4. Requisition Approval
       ↓
5. Purchase Order
       ↓
6. PO Approval
       ↓
7. Vendor Confirmation
       ↓
8. Goods Receipt / Service Entry
       ↓
9. Vendor Bill / Invoice
       ↓
10. Three-Way Matching
       ↓
11. Payment
       ↓
12. Accounting Close
```

## Departments Involved

| Department | Role in Procurement |
|---|---|
| **Requesting Department** | Identifies the need, creates requisition |
| **Purchasing / Procurement** | Selects vendors, negotiates terms, creates POs |
| **Legal** | Reviews contracts and terms |
| **Finance / Accounting** | Approves budgets, processes invoices, issues payments |
| **Receiving / Warehouse** | Receives and inspects goods |
| **Accounts Payable** | Processes vendor bills, schedules payments |
| **Management** | Approves purchases above thresholds |

## Why Procurement Matters

| Reason | Impact |
|---|---|
| **Cost control** | Procurement represents 50-80% of revenue for many businesses |
| **Compliance** | Regulatory requirements for purchasing processes |
| **Fraud prevention** | Segregation of duties prevents unauthorized spending |
| **Cash flow management** | Payment terms affect working capital |
| **Supplier relationships** | Consistent processes build vendor trust |
| **Operational efficiency** | Well-managed procurement prevents stockouts and delays |

## Procurement vs. Purchasing

| Procurement | Purchasing |
|---|---|
| Strategic — vendor selection, contract negotiation, relationship management | Tactical — PO creation, order placement, transaction processing |
| Long-term — supplier development, category management | Day-to-day — order fulfillment, receipt processing |
| Cross-functional — involves legal, finance, operations | Operational — executed by purchasing agents |
| Includes sourcing, contracting, and supplier management | Focuses on the transactional part of the process |

## NetSuite and Procurement

NetSuite supports procurement through:

- **Vendor records** — Master data for suppliers
- **Purchase Requisitions** — Internal requests for goods and services
- **Purchase Orders** — The formal commitment to purchase
- **Approval Workflows** — Automated routing for authorization
- **Item Receipt** — Recording goods received (see [Inventory](../inventory/receiving-inventory.md))
- **Vendor Bills** — Supplier invoices for payment
- **Three-Way Matching** — PO, receipt, and invoice reconciliation
- **Payment Processing** — Check, ACH, credit card, or electronic payment

## Business Example

A manufacturing company needs to procure raw materials for the next production run:

1. **Need identified**: Production planner determines that 500 kg of Material X is needed
2. **Vendor selected**: Preferred vendor ABC Chemicals is chosen based on contracted pricing
3. **Requisition**: Production manager creates a purchase requisition for 500 kg
4. **Approval**: Requisition is automatically routed to the plant manager for approval
5. **PO issued**: Purchasing creates a PO for 500 kg at $12/kg = $6,000
6. **Vendor confirmation**: ABC Chemicals acknowledges the order
7. **Receipt**: Warehouse receives 500 kg, creates an Item Receipt
8. **Bill**: ABC Chemicals invoices $6,000
9. **Three-way match**: PO, receipt, and invoice quantities and prices match
10. **Payment**: AP processes payment on net-30 terms

## Common Mistakes

- **Treating procurement as an administrative task**: Strategic procurement adds significant value through vendor negotiation and supply chain optimization
- **No segregation of duties**: The person who creates the PO should not also receive the goods or approve the invoice
- **Skipping the requisition step**: Without requisitions, purchases happen without budget verification or management visibility
- **Ignoring procurement data**: PO history, vendor performance, and spend analysis provide valuable business intelligence

## Best Practices

- Establish a clear procurement policy with defined approval thresholds
- Segregate duties across requisitioning, purchasing, receiving, and payment
- Use purchase orders for all vendor commitments — no PO, no payment
- Implement three-way matching to verify invoice accuracy
- Review vendor performance quarterly
- Monitor procurement cycle time and identify bottlenecks
