# Purchasing Glossary

## Quick Summary

A reference of terms used in procurement and purchasing in NetSuite.

## A

**Accounts Payable (AP)**
The department and ledger account responsible for processing vendor bills and issuing payments.

**Approval Workflow**
An automated process that routes purchase documents to designated approvers based on defined criteria.

## B

**Blanket Purchase Order**
A long-term PO that establishes pricing and terms for recurring purchases. Specific quantities are ordered through releases against the blanket.

## D

**Drop Ship**
A fulfillment method where the vendor ships ordered goods directly to the company's customer. The company never handles the inventory.

**Due Date**
The date by which a vendor bill must be paid based on the agreed payment terms.

## I

**Item Receipt**
A transaction that records goods received from a vendor against a purchase order. Increases inventory and creates an accrued purchase liability.

## M

**Maestro Spend**
The percentage of total spend with preferred or contracted vendors. Higher maestro spend indicates better procurement management.

## N

**Non-PO Spend**
Spend that occurs without a purchase order. Typically requires higher-level approval because it bypasses normal controls.

## P

**Payment Terms**
The agreed conditions for payment, including the due date and any early payment discounts.

**Procure-to-Pay (P2P)**
The end-to-end process from identifying a purchasing need through making payment to the vendor.

**Purchase Order (PO)**
A legally binding document authorizing a vendor to provide goods or services at specified prices and terms.

**Purchase Requisition**
An internal request for goods or services that initiates the procurement process before a PO is created.

## R

**Release**
A specific order placed against a blanket purchase order.

**Request for Proposal (RFP)**
A formal solicitation asking vendors to submit proposals for goods or services.

## S

**Segregation of Duties**
A control principle requiring that no single person controls all stages of a transaction. In purchasing, PO creation, approval, receiving, and payment should be handled by different people.

**Special Order**
A purchase order created specifically to fulfill a customer sales order. The item is not normally stocked.

**Standard Purchase Order**
A one-time purchase order for a specific quantity of goods or services from a vendor.

## T

**Three-Way Matching**
The process of comparing the purchase order, item receipt, and vendor bill to verify quantities and prices before payment.

**Two-Way Matching**
The process of comparing the purchase order and vendor bill without requiring an item receipt. Used for services and non-inventory items.

## V

**Vendor**
A company or individual from whom goods or services are purchased.

**Vendor Bill**
An invoice received from a vendor requesting payment for goods or services provided. Also referred to as a vendor invoice.

**Vendor Record**
The master data record containing a vendor's legal name, address, tax ID, payment terms, and contact information.
