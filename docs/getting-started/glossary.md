# NetSuite Glossary

## Quick Summary

A reference of common NetSuite terms, acronyms, and concepts. Use this glossary when you encounter an unfamiliar term in other articles.

## Terms

### A

**Account (NetSuite Account)**
The top-level instance of NetSuite for an organization. An account has its own URL, configuration, data, users, and roles.

**Administrator**
A user role with full access to configuration, users, roles, customization, and all data in the account.

**Advanced Inventory**
A NetSuite module that adds bin management, lot tracking, serial number tracking, and matrix items to standard inventory management.

**Assembly Item**
An item type used in manufacturing. An assembly item is built from component items using a bill of materials (BOM).

**Audit Trail**
A record of changes made to records, transactions, roles, and permissions in NetSuite. Used for compliance and troubleshooting.

### B

**Bill of Materials (BOM)**
A list of components, quantities, and steps needed to build an assembly item.

**Bin**
A specific storage location within a warehouse (e.g., "Aisle 3, Shelf 4, Bin 12"). Used with bin management features.

**Build Order**
A transaction that authorizes the assembly of an item from its component parts.

### C

**Center**
A navigational framework in NetSuite that groups menus, dashboards, and pages around a business function (e.g., Home, Sales, Inventory).

**CRM (Customer Relationship Management)**
Tools for managing customer interactions, including lead tracking, campaign management, and support cases.

**CSV Import**
A feature for importing or updating records in bulk using comma-separated value files.

**Custom Field**
A user-defined field added to a standard or custom record type. Custom fields let organizations store data specific to their business.

**Custom Record**
A user-defined record type that stores data not covered by standard record types.

**Custom Segment**
A classification field that can be applied across multiple record types.

### D

**Dashboard**
A customizable landing page in NetSuite that displays portlets such as KPIs, reminders, saved searches, and reports.

**Department**
A classification used for reporting and assignment. Departments can be assigned to transactions, records, and employees.

### E

**ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning)**
A software system that integrates core business processes — accounting, inventory, orders, CRM, and more — into a shared platform. NetSuite is an ERP system.

**Estimated Time**
The typical duration listed at the top of articles to help readers plan their time.

### F

**Form**
The layout of fields and tabs used to view or edit a record. Forms can be customized per role.

**Front Matter**
YAML metadata at the top of a Markdown file containing title, module, difficulty, status, and other structured information.

**Fulfillment**
The process of picking, packing, and shipping items to fill a sales order.

### G

**General Ledger (GL)**
The central record of all financial transactions in NetSuite. Every transaction with financial impact posts to the GL.

**Global Search**
The quick-search field in the top-right corner of NetSuite that finds records by name, number, or keyword.

**Governance (SuiteScript)**
The usage limit system in SuiteScript that restricts how many script operations can run in a single execution.

### I

**Internal ID**
A unique system-generated number assigned to every NetSuite record (e.g., 12345). Used in scripting, integrations, and reporting.

**Invoice**
A transaction requesting payment for goods or services provided.

**Item**
A record representing a product, service, or other billable entity. Item types include inventory, non-inventory, service, kit, and assembly.

### J

**Journal Entry**
A manual accounting transaction that debits and credits general ledger accounts.

### K

**KPI (Key Performance Indicator)**
A measurable value displayed on dashboards, such as revenue, open orders, or cash balance.

### L

**Location**
A physical or logical place where inventory is stored, such as a warehouse, store, or dock.

**Lot Number**
A number assigned to a batch of items, used for traceability. Managed through the Advanced Inventory module.

### M

**Map/Reduce Script**
A SuiteScript type that processes large datasets in parallel batches.

**Multi-Book Accounting**
A feature that allows running multiple accounting books (e.g., GAAP, tax, IFRS) in parallel.

### O

**OneWorld**
NetSuite's enterprise edition for organizations with multiple subsidiaries, currencies, and international operations.

### P

**Permission**
A setting within a role that controls access to a specific feature, record type, transaction, or report.

**Portlet**
A small panel on a NetSuite dashboard that displays a specific type of information (KPI, saved search, calendar, etc.).

**Purchase Order (PO)**
A transaction sent to a vendor to order goods or services.

### R

**Record**
A single data entry in NetSuite, such as a customer, item, vendor, or transaction.

**Record Type**
The category of a record, which determines its fields, forms, and behavior. Examples: Customer, Sales Order, Item.

**RESTlet**
A SuiteScript type that creates custom REST endpoints.

**Role**
A collection of permissions that determines what a user can access and do in NetSuite.

### S

**Sales Order (SO)**
A transaction recording a customer's order for goods or services.

**Saved Search**
A reusable search with configured criteria, results, and filters. Saved searches are a primary reporting tool in NetSuite.

**Serial Number**
A unique identifier assigned to an individual item for tracking and warranty purposes.

**SuiteAnalytics**
NetSuite's advanced analytics platform for creating custom datasets and workbooks.

**SuiteCloud**
The collective name for NetSuite's development platform, including SuiteScript, SuiteTalk, SDF, and related tools.

**SuiteScript**
NetSuite's JavaScript-based scripting platform for customization and automation.

**SuiteTalk**
NetSuite's API platform, including REST Web Services and SOAP web services.

**Subsidiary**
A legal entity within a OneWorld account. Each subsidiary can have its own currency, tax rules, and financial reporting.

### T

**Transaction**
A record with financial or inventory impact, such as a sales order, invoice, purchase order, or inventory transfer.

**Transfer Order**
A tracked inventory movement between locations with stages (pending, shipped, received, in transit).

### U

**User Event Script**
A SuiteScript type that runs when a record is loaded, submitted, or deleted.

### V

**Vendor**
A company or individual from whom you purchase goods or services.

### W

**Workflow**
A configurable automation that triggers actions based on record changes, such as sending approval requests or updating fields.

**WMS (Warehouse Management System)**
A NetSuite module for advanced warehouse operations, including directed putaway, wave picking, and RF scanning.
