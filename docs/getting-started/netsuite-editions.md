# NetSuite Editions

## Quick Summary

NetSuite is offered in several editions that determine which features and modules are available to your account. The most common editions are Limited, Standard, and OneWorld, with additional modules available for specific industries and use cases.

## Difficulty

Beginner

## Estimated Time

5 minutes

## Overview

Not every NetSuite account has access to the same features. The edition your organization licenses determines which modules, features, and transaction types are available. Understanding which edition you are on helps you know what is possible without custom development.

## Common Editions

### NetSuite Limited (formerly Starter)

A simplified edition designed for smaller businesses. It includes core accounting, CRM, and order management but has fewer customization options and lower transaction limits.

**Typical for**: Small businesses new to ERP, simple operations, single-subsidiary companies.

**Key limitations**: Fewer customization options, limited advanced features, no SuiteScript or SuiteTalk access in some configurations.

### NetSuite Standard

The most common edition for mid-market businesses. Includes full accounting, CRM, inventory, order management, reporting, and customization capabilities. SuiteScript, SuiteTalk, and SuiteAnalytics are available.

**Typical for**: Growing businesses with moderate complexity, multi-department operations, need for customization and integration.

### NetSuite OneWorld

The enterprise edition designed for businesses with multiple subsidiaries, currencies, and international operations. Adds multi-book accounting, intercompany transactions, currency management, and consolidated reporting.

**Typical for**: Multinational companies, holding companies, businesses with multiple legal entities, organizations needing multi-currency support.

## Add-On Modules

Beyond the base editions, NetSuite offers optional modules that can be added to any edition (where supported):

| Module | Description |
|---|---|
| Advanced Inventory | Bin management, lot tracking, serial tracking, matrix items |
| WMS | Warehouse management with RF scanning, wave picking, directed putaway |
| SuiteCommerce | Ecommerce website and customer portal |
| SuiteCloud Platform | SuiteScript, SuiteTalk, SuiteCloud Development Framework |
| SuiteAnalytics | Workbook creation, custom datasets, SQL-based querying |
| Fixed Assets Management | Asset tracking, depreciation, disposal |
| Revenue Management | Revenue recognition, allocation, ASC 606 compliance |
| Multi-Book Accounting | Run multiple accounting books (GAAP, tax, IFRS) in parallel |
| Payroll (Partners) | Integrated payroll through NetSuite SuiteApps partners |

## Industry-Specific Editions

NetSuite also offers editions tailored to specific industries:

- **NetSuite for Services** — Professional services automation, project management, billing
- **NetSuite for Wholesale Distribution** — Advanced inventory, demand planning, drop shipping
- **NetSuite for Retail** — Omnichannel, point-of-sale, ecommerce integration
- **NetSuite for Manufacturing** — Work orders, BOMs, demand planning, production management
- **NetSuite for Software** — Recurring billing, subscription management, revenue recognition

## How to Tell Which Edition You Are On

1. Navigate to **Setup > Company > Company Information**
2. Look for the account type or edition field
3. If you see references to "OneWorld," you are on the enterprise edition

Your NetSuite account administrator or Oracle account manager can confirm the exact edition and licensed modules.

## Common Mistakes

- **Assuming a feature exists because it is mentioned in documentation**: Many SuiteAnswers articles and official docs describe features that require specific editions or add-on modules.
- **Purchasing the wrong edition for your needs**: A small business with multi-currency needs may outgrow Limited edition quickly.
- **Not checking license entitlements before building a SuiteScript solution**: Some automation needs can be met by configuring a standard feature that is already included in your edition.

## Best Practices

- Know your edition and licensed modules before planning projects.
- Check SuiteAnswers or the NetSuite Help Center for edition-specific documentation.
- Work with your Oracle account manager to understand which modules would add value before purchasing.
- Start with standard features before building custom solutions.
