# What is NetSuite?

## Quick Summary

NetSuite is a cloud-based business management platform used to run core business processes such as accounting, sales, purchasing, inventory, reporting, customer management, ecommerce, and operations.

## Difficulty

Beginner

## Estimated Time

5 minutes

## Overview

NetSuite is commonly described as an enterprise resource planning system, or ERP. An ERP connects major business functions into one shared system so that teams can work from the same records, transactions, and reports.

Instead of managing accounting in one tool, inventory in another, and sales orders in a third, NetSuite is designed to centralize those processes.

## Why It Matters

A shared system reduces duplicate data entry, improves reporting, and gives teams a more complete view of the business. For example, a sales order can connect to inventory availability, fulfillment, invoicing, customer records, and financial reporting.

## Common NetSuite Areas

- Accounting and financials
- Customer relationship management
- Sales orders and invoicing
- Purchasing and receiving
- Inventory management
- Manufacturing
- Reporting and analytics
- Workflows and automation
- SuiteScript development
- SuiteTalk integrations

## Best Practices

- Learn the basic record and transaction types before learning advanced features.
- Understand your role and permissions because the NetSuite interface changes based on access.
- Use global search to quickly find records.
- Treat dashboards and saved searches as daily work tools, not just reporting features.

## Common Mistakes

- Assuming every user sees the same menus.
- Confusing records with transactions.
- Making configuration changes without understanding downstream accounting or operational effects.
- Treating NetSuite as only accounting software instead of a broader business platform.
