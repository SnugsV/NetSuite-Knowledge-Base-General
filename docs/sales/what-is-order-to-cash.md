# What Is Order-to-Cash?

## Quick Summary

Order-to-Cash (O2C) is the end-to-end business process of receiving and fulfilling customer orders, invoicing customers, and collecting payment. It is the primary revenue-generating process in any organization.

## Difficulty

Beginner

## Estimated Time

10 minutes

## Overview

Order-to-Cash is the mirror of Procure-to-Pay. Where P2P covers spending money to acquire goods, O2C covers selling goods to collect money. Together, they form the two core operational cycles of any business that buys and sells products.

## The O2C Lifecycle

```
Lead Generation
       ↓
Quote / Proposal
       ↓
Sales Order
       ↓
Credit Check (if applicable)
       ↓
Inventory Allocation
       ↓
Picking
       ↓
Packing
       ↓
Shipping
       ↓
Invoice
       ↓
Payment Collection
       ↓
Revenue Recognition
```

## Departments Involved

| Department | Role in O2C |
|---|---|
| **Sales / Account Management** | Customer relationships, quoting, order entry |
| **Customer Service** | Order status, inquiries, issue resolution |
| **Credit / Finance** | Credit checks, payment terms |
| **Warehouse / Fulfillment** | Picking, packing, shipping |
| **Shipping / Logistics** | Carrier selection, tracking |
| **Accounts Receivable** | Invoicing, payment processing, collections |
| **Accounting** | Revenue recognition, financial reporting |

## Why O2C Matters

| Reason | Impact |
|---|---|
| **Revenue generation** | O2C is how the company gets paid |
| **Customer satisfaction** | Smooth O2C processes retain customers |
| **Cash flow** | Faster O2C cycles improve working capital |
| **Compliance** | Revenue recognition rules affect financial reporting |
| **Data quality** | Customer and order data drives analytics |

## O2C vs. Procure-to-Pay

| Dimension | O2C | P2P |
|---|---|---|
| **Direction** | Outbound — selling to customers | Inbound — buying from vendors |
| **Core document** | Sales Order | Purchase Order |
| **Inventory effect** | Decreases inventory | Increases inventory |
| **Financial impact** | Revenue, AR, cash inflow | Expense, AP, cash outflow |
| **Customer/Vendor** | Customer is external | Vendor is external |
| **Key departments** | Sales, fulfillment, AR | Purchasing, receiving, AP |

## NetSuite and O2C

NetSuite supports the O2C lifecycle through:

- **Customer records** — Master data for buyers
- **Sales Orders** — The core transaction committing to a sale
- **Item Fulfillment** — Recording shipment of goods
- **Invoices** — Requesting payment from customers
- **Customer Payments** — Recording cash received
- **Return Authorizations** — Handling customer returns
- **Revenue Recognition** — Deferring or recognizing revenue per accounting rules

## Business Example

A distributor receives a customer order:

1. **Sales order**: Customer service enters a sales order for 100 units of Product A at $15 each
2. **Credit check**: System verifies the customer's credit limit is sufficient
3. **Inventory allocation**: 100 units are committed from available stock
4. **Pick**: Warehouse team picks 100 units from bins
5. **Pack**: Items are packed into shipping boxes
6. **Ship**: Carrier picks up, tracking number recorded
7. **Invoice**: System generates an invoice for $1,500
8. **Payment**: Customer pays on Net 30 terms
9. **Revenue**: Revenue is recognized when criteria are met

## Common Mistakes

- **Incomplete customer data**: Missing contact, billing, or shipping information causes order delays
- **No credit check process**: Extending credit without verification increases AR risk
- **Poor inventory visibility**: Selling items that are not available creates backorders and customer frustration
- **Delayed fulfillment**: Orders not fulfilled promptly lead to cancellations and lost revenue
- **Manual invoice processing**: Manual invoicing is error-prone and slow

## Best Practices

- Maintain clean customer master data
- Automate credit checks on sales orders
- Integrate order management with inventory availability
- Establish clear fulfillment SLAs
- Automate invoicing from fulfilled orders
- Monitor O2C cycle time and identify bottlenecks
