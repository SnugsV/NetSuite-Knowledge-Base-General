# Manufacturing Costing

## Quick Summary

Manufacturing costing determines how the cost of production is calculated — material costs, labor costs, overhead allocation, and variance analysis. The costing method affects inventory valuation, COGS, and profitability analysis.

## Difficulty

Advanced

## Estimated Time

20 minutes

## Business Question

"What did it actually cost to produce this product?"

## Overview

Manufacturing costing is more complex than distribution costing because costs are not simply passed through. Raw materials are transformed, labor is added, overhead is allocated. Each element must be tracked and valued.

## Cost Components

| Component | Source | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Direct Materials** | Component costs from BOM | Frame $25, Fabric $10, Foam $8 |
| **Direct Labor** | Routing run times × labor rate | 12 min at $30/hr = $6 |
| **Manufacturing Overhead** | Allocated costs (utilities, rent, supervision) | 150% of direct labor = $9 |
| **Total Unit Cost** | Sum of above | $49 |

## Standard Costing

Standard costing assigns a fixed cost to each component and operation. Actual costs are compared to standard costs, and variances are recorded.

| Variance Type | What It Measures |
|---|---|
| **Material Price Variance** | Actual purchase price vs. standard cost |
| **Material Usage Variance** | Actual quantity used vs. BOM quantity |
| **Labor Rate Variance** | Actual labor rate vs. standard rate |
| **Labor Efficiency Variance** | Actual hours vs. standard hours |
| **Overhead Variance** | Applied overhead vs. actual overhead |

## Average Costing

With average costing, component costs are based on the current average cost of each inventory item. The assembly cost is the sum of the component averages at the time of build.

## Which Method to Choose

| Characteristic | Standard Cost | Average Cost |
|---|---|---|
| **Stability** | Costs are predictable | Costs fluctuate with purchases |
| **Variance tracking** | Variance accounts highlight issues | No variance visibility |
| **Complexity** | Requires regular standard cost updates | Minimal maintenance |
| **Best for** | Manufacturing with stable processes | Simple assembly |
| **Variance management** | Required | Not applicable |

## Common Customer Questions

**Q**: The assembly cost looks wrong. Why? **Records to Inspect**: Component costs, BOM quantities, work order quantities, routing setup, overhead allocation percentages.

**Q**: Variance is growing every period. What is happening? **Records to Inspect**: Purchase prices vs. standard costs, component usage vs. BOM, labor times vs. routing standards.
