# What Is Manufacturing?

## Quick Summary

Manufacturing is the process of transforming raw materials into finished goods using labor, equipment, and defined processes. In NetSuite, manufacturing is managed through bills of materials, work orders, routings, and costing methods that track value from raw material to finished product.

## Overview

Manufacturing is where inventory is not just stored and moved — it is transformed. Raw materials enter production, are combined and modified through defined processes, and emerge as finished goods with a different identity, value, and cost structure.

## The Plan-to-Produce Lifecycle

```
Demand Forecast / Sales Order
       ↓
Production Plan
       ↓
Material Requirements Planning
       ↓
Raw Material Procurement
       ↓
Work Order Created
       ↓
Components Issued to Production
       ↓
Production Execution (Routings)
       ↓
Quality Inspection
       ↓
Finished Goods Received
       ↓
Cost Variance Analysis
       ↓
Inventory Valuation Update
```

## Manufacturing vs. Distribution

| Dimension | Distribution | Manufacturing |
|---|---|---|
| **Core activity** | Buy, store, sell | Transform raw materials into finished goods |
| **Inventory types** | One type — finished goods | Multiple types — raw materials, WIP, finished goods |
| **Cost complexity** | Purchase price + landed cost | Raw materials + labor + overhead |
| **Key documents** | PO, SO, Transfer | BOM, Work Order, Routing |
| **Key features** | Multi-Location, Bins, Transfers | BOM, Work Order, Backflushing, Routings |

## Business Example

A furniture manufacturer buys lumber, fabric, and hardware. These raw materials are transformed through cutting, assembly, finishing, and packaging into a finished chair. The value of the chair is not simply the sum of its components — it includes the labor and overhead of the production process.
