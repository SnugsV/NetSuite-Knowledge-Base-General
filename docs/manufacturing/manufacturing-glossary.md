# Manufacturing Glossary

## Quick Summary

Key terms used in manufacturing within NetSuite.

## A

**Assembly Item**
An item type representing a product built from components using a BOM.

## B

**Backflushing**
Automatic consumption of components when a work order is built, based on BOM quantities.

**Bill of Materials (BOM)**
The list of components and quantities required to build one unit of an assembly item.

## C

**Component**
A raw material, sub-assembly, or part used in the production of an assembly item.

## F

**First Pass Yield**
The percentage of units passing inspection on the first attempt without rework.

## M

**Manufacturing Overhead**
Indirect production costs allocated to products (utilities, rent, supervision).

## P

**Phantom BOM**
A BOM for a sub-assembly that is built during production but not stocked separately.

## R

**Routing**
The sequence of production steps, with work center, time, and labor requirements.

## S

**Scrap**
Material that is wasted during production and cannot be used or reworked.

**Standard Cost**
A predetermined cost assigned to materials, labor, and overhead for costing purposes.

## W

**Work Center**
A specific machine, production line, or area where manufacturing operations are performed.

**Work in Progress (WIP)**
The value of components and labor that have been issued to production but not yet completed.

**Work Order**
The document authorizing the production of a specific quantity of an assembly item.
